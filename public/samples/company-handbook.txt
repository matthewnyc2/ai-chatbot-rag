Acme Corp Employee Handbook - 2024 Edition

Section 1: Company Overview

Acme Corp was founded in 2010 by Sarah Chen and Marcus Rivera with a mission to democratize access to AI-powered tools for small businesses. Headquartered in Austin, Texas, the company has grown to over 500 employees across 3 offices in the United States, United Kingdom, and Singapore.

Our core values are: Innovation First, Customer Obsession, Radical Transparency, and Continuous Learning.

Section 2: Work Schedule and Remote Policy

Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM local time, Monday through Friday. Acme Corp operates on a hybrid work model. Employees are expected to be in the office at least 2 days per week (Tuesday and Thursday are company-wide in-office days). Remote work is permitted for the remaining days with manager approval.

Core collaboration hours are 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM local time, during which all employees should be available for meetings and synchronous communication.

Section 3: Paid Time Off (PTO)

All full-time employees receive the following PTO benefits:
- 20 days of vacation per year (accrued monthly)
- 10 sick days per year
- 5 personal days per year
- 12 company holidays
- 2 floating holidays

PTO requests should be submitted through the HR portal at least 2 weeks in advance for planned absences. Unused vacation days can be carried over up to a maximum of 5 days into the following year.

Section 4: Health and Benefits

Acme Corp provides comprehensive health benefits starting on the first day of employment:
- Medical insurance (Blue Cross Blue Shield PPO and HMO options)
- Dental insurance (Delta Dental)
- Vision insurance (VSP)
- Life insurance (2x annual salary)
- 401(k) with 4% company match (vesting after 1 year)
- Health Savings Account (HSA) with $500 annual company contribution
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) with free counseling sessions

Section 5: Professional Development

Acme Corp invests in employee growth through:
- Annual learning stipend of $2,500 per employee
- Access to LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, and O'Reilly platforms
- Internal mentorship program
- Quarterly hackathons
- Conference attendance budget of $3,000 per year
- Tuition reimbursement up to $10,000 per year for approved programs

Section 6: Performance Reviews

Performance reviews are conducted semi-annually in June and December. The review process includes:
1. Self-assessment by the employee
2. Peer feedback from 3-5 colleagues
3. Manager evaluation
4. Calibration sessions across teams
5. One-on-one discussion with direct manager

Compensation adjustments are made annually in January based on performance, market data, and company performance.

Section 7: Technology and Equipment

New employees receive:
- Choice of MacBook Pro or Dell XPS laptop
- External monitor (up to 32 inches)
- Keyboard and mouse of choice
- $500 home office stipend (one-time)
- Monthly internet stipend of $50 for remote work

All company equipment must be returned upon separation from the company. Employees are responsible for maintaining the security of their devices and must report any lost or stolen equipment to IT within 24 hours.

Section 8: Code of Conduct

Employees are expected to maintain professional behavior at all times. This includes:
- Treating all colleagues with respect and dignity
- Avoiding conflicts of interest
- Protecting confidential company information
- Complying with all applicable laws and regulations
- Reporting any concerns through the ethics hotline (anonymous reporting available)

Violations of the code of conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Section 9: Travel Policy

Business travel must be pre-approved by the employee's manager. Guidelines include:
- Domestic flights: Economy class for flights under 4 hours, Economy Plus for longer flights
- International flights: Business class for flights over 8 hours
- Hotel: Up to $200/night for domestic travel, up to $300/night for international travel
- Meals: Up to $75/day (receipts required for expenses over $25)
- Ground transportation: Use of rideshare services preferred; rental cars require advance approval

Section 10: Separation Policy

Voluntary separation requires a minimum 2-week notice period (4 weeks for senior roles). Upon separation, employees will receive their final paycheck within the timeframe required by state law, including payment for any unused PTO. COBRA health insurance continuation is available for up to 18 months.
